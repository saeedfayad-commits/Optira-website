Educate and inspire children aged 5–12 across core subjects, shaping how they think and learn.

A day in the life

  • Plan and deliver lessons across subjects like literacy, numeracy and science
  • Manage classroom behaviour and adapt teaching to different learning needs
  • Mark work, track student progress and write reports
  • Communicate with parents/carers about how each child is going

"No two days look the same. One minute you're explaining fractions, the next you're mediating a playground disagreement. It's exhausting some weeks — but watching a kid finally 'get' something they've struggled with all term is why I stayed in it."

— composite quote based on common experiences shared by NSW primary teachers

How to get there (NSW pathway)

1
HSC
English, plus any two other subjects — breadth matters more than a specific pattern.
2
Study
Bachelor of Education (Primary) — 4 years, or a relevant degree + Master of Teaching (2 years).
3
Get qualified
Register with NESA (NSW Education Standards Authority) to gain accreditation and work in NSW schools.

Is this you?

You might love this if

  • You enjoy explaining things until they click for someone else
  • You want a job with real, visible day-to-day impact
  • You're comfortable being "on" in front of a group for hours
  • You like variety — no two days are identical

It might be tough if

  • You dislike marking and admin outside school hours
  • Managing 25+ kids' behaviour at once sounds draining, not energising
  • You want a job that switches off when you leave the building
  • Dealing with parent concerns and expectations feels stressful to you
