Government Support

Financial help from the government.

Understand what support is available to help you study, train, and build your career.

💰
Youth Allowance
  • Available for full-time students aged 16–24
  • Up to $512.50 per fortnight for single students living away from home
  • Means-tested based on parental income
  • Apply through Services Australia (Centrelink)
📚
Study Support Payments
  • HECS-HELP: government-subsidised university places with deferred repayment
  • FEE-HELP: loans for full-fee paying students
  • SA-HELP: covers student services and amenities fees
  • Repayments start when income exceeds ~$54,000
🔧
Apprenticeships Support
  • Australian Apprenticeships Incentives for priority occupations
  • Trade Support Loans up to $21,078 over the life of the apprenticeship
  • Living away from home allowance for rural apprentices
  • Tools and equipment allowances in some states
🎓
Education Assistance
  • ABSTUDY for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander students
  • Assistance for Isolated Children Scheme (rural and remote students)
  • Pensioner Education Supplement
  • Education Entry Payment for eligible welfare recipients starting study
🆓
Free Learning Programs
  • Fee-Free TAFE: thousands of free vocational courses in priority areas
  • JobTrainer: free or low-fee courses for job seekers aged 17–24
  • Skills Checkpoint program for career planning support
  • State-based free training initiatives (vary by state)
🌟
Grants for Disadvantaged Students
  • Start-up Scholarships for Centrelink recipients commencing study
  • Relocation scholarships for students moving to study
  • Indigenous Advancement Strategy grants
  • State-based equity scholarships and bursaries

⚠️ Government programs and payment rates may change. Always check the official Services Australia or relevant state government website for the latest information and eligibility criteria.
